Why this year matters

1999 is the moment the 90s stops pretending it can stay stable. The web starts breaking old business models, Hollywood starts talking about simulation and marketing mythology, and everyone is weirdly aware of the clock.

It is the most "end of an era" year in the site. Burned CDs, message boards, dot-com jobs, silver plastic gadgets, and chain emails all feel normal together. The 2000s are already visible — but they still arrive through CRTs, CD players, and modem sounds.
