Why this year matters

1996 is when the decade starts behaving like a social desktop: friend codes, guestbooks, toy collecting, soundtrack summers, and 3D games all become things you swap, compare, and carry around with other people.

The year still belongs to malls, radio, VHS trailers, and food-court gravity, but the web finally feels less like a library and more like a neighborhood with handles, counters, and inside jokes.
