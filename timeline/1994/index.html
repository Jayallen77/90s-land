Why this year matters

1994 is when the web starts to look like a place, not just a protocol. Human-made directories, browser downloads, fan pages, and counters turn online life into something ordinary people can imagine using for fun.

Offline, the year is absurdly stacked: punk-pop goes mall-wide, New York rap gets canonical, indie-cool film goes mainstream, sitcom hangout energy hardens into comfort TV, and game hardware starts tilting toward CDs and 3D.
