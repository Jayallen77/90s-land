Late-decade briefing

1998 made the future feel playful, searchable, and slightly weird.

A cleaner search box appeared just as the web was getting impossible to manually map. Apple made the family computer look like a piece of candy. Games delivered all-time 3D classics. Kids wanted a Furby, a Game Boy link cable, Nickelodeon slime, and maybe their own tiny homepage with a visitor counter.

Major world / cultural events

  • The Good Friday Agreement marked a major peace milestone in Northern Ireland.
  • The Clinton-Lewinsky scandal dominated U.S. media and helped turn cable news into an always-on spectacle.
  • Dot-com confidence kept building, with the internet increasingly treated as business future, media subject, and social curiosity at once.
  • Design culture leaned toward chrome, plastic, rounded shapes, inflatable furniture, and an almost toy-like version of the future.

Music releases + moments

  • Lauryn Hill’s The Miseducation of Lauryn Hill became one of the decade’s defining albums across hip-hop, soul, and pop.
  • OutKast, Beastie Boys, Madonna, Fatboy Slim, Massive Attack, Garbage, and DMX showed how wide the late-90s soundscape had become.
  • MTV’s Total Request Live launched, turning fan voting, countdowns, and screaming sidewalk crowds into a daily pop ritual.
  • MP3 culture was still rough around the edges, but the idea of music as files was moving closer to ordinary life.

Movies + TV

  • The Truman Show made mediated reality feel uncanny before webcams, influencer culture, and social feeds normalized being watched.
  • Saving Private Ryan, Blade, Mulan, Rush Hour, and The Big Lebowski show how broad one theater year could be.
  • Dawson’s Creek and Sex and the City helped define late-90s TV conversation, style, and relationship vocabulary.
  • Nickelodeon, MTV, Cartoon Network, cable reruns, and VHS rentals still created shared viewing habits before streaming.

Games

  • The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time became a landmark for 3D adventure, lock-on combat, and cinematic console pacing.
  • Half-Life, Metal Gear Solid, StarCraft, Resident Evil 2, and Spyro the Dragon made 1998 one of gaming’s stacked years.
  • Pokémon’s wider rollout began turning link cables, playground trades, strategy guides, and Game Boy screens into a social network.
  • PC gaming meant demos, patches, graphics cards, LAN dreams, and reading install notes like you were defusing a bomb.

Tech / internet milestones

  • Google was founded and pointed toward a cleaner, faster way to navigate an increasingly chaotic web.
  • Apple introduced the Bondi blue iMac, making the home computer friendlier, brighter, and less beige.
  • The web was still handmade enough for fan shrines and personal pages, but large portals, search engines, and online shopping were becoming normal landmarks.
  • AIM buddy lists, email forwards, forum handles, and early personal websites made online identity feel casual and experimental.

Everyday life / trends

  • Furby arrived as a holiday-season animatronic obsession: cute, unsettling, and instantly rumor-powered.
  • Transparent and translucent plastic spread across gadgets, controllers, phones, toys, and school supplies.
  • Malls, music stores, movie theaters, arcades, and Blockbuster shelves still acted as the weekend discovery engine.
  • School computer labs, CD-ROM encyclopedias, printed web pages, and slow downloads made digital life feel special because it was not always available.

Season-by-season capsule

Winter / spring

  • Teen TV, cable countdowns, and movie soundtracks helped set the late-90s mood.
  • Online life was still framed around portals, directories, search boxes, and word-of-mouth URLs.

Summer / fall

  • The iMac made translucent design feel like the future had been put on a dorm desk.
  • Google incorporated in September, quietly setting up the next web era.
  • Holiday lists filled with Furby, Game Boy cartridges, strategy guides, CDs, and anything see-through.

Museum label

If 1995 was “install the future,” 1998 was “decorate it.”

The decade’s technology stopped looking only corporate and started looking personal: candy shell computers, pocket creatures, screen names, buddy lists, search engines, and game worlds that felt like places you could live inside.
