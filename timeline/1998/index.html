Why this year matters

1998 is when the late-90s interface becomes obvious: search starts beating directories, computers start selling themselves on vibe, and kid culture moves at MTV speed.

It is one of the decade’s densest crossover years. You can track it through school supplies, CD binders, Pokémon link cables, slim jewel cases, and the exact shade of blue used on the first iMac. Everything feels brighter, more branded, and more instantly shareable — but still tactile enough to carry in a backpack.

Fast read

Why 1998 is so easy to recognize

  • Search gets simpler: Google turns finding things into a cleaner habit instead of a directory scavenger hunt.
  • Hardware gets photogenic: Bondi blue and translucent shells make the desk itself part of the nostalgia.
  • TV gets daily: TRL turns after-school music fandom into a real-time ritual.
  • Kid culture gets portable: link cables, handhelds, guides, and toy panic travel between house, mall, and school.
