Why this year matters

1995 is when the 90s stop previewing the future and start selling it to regular people. The browser becomes a brand, the computer gets a Start button, and the web starts sounding like a household noun instead of lab slang.

It is also a giant mass-culture year: Toy Story proves CGI can carry a whole movie, PlayStation starts changing how games look and feel in the U.S., and even shopping starts getting a weird little online doorway.
