Style briefing

90s style was not one look. It was scenes colliding in the food court.

Music videos, sitcom wardrobes, skate shops, club flyers, magazines, school hallways, and mall stores all pushed different uniforms at once.

GRNG

Grunge thrift

Flannel, band tees, worn denim, Doc Martens, messy layers, and the anti-polish look that became a mall product anyway.

pair with: 1991 + Music
HIP

Hip-hop streetwear

Oversized denim, jerseys, tracksuits, Timberland-style boots, logo heat, bucket hats, and regional style codes from videos and magazines.

pair with: mixtapes + TV
RAVE

Rave color

Reflective fabric, tiny backpacks, candy bracelets, platform shoes, visors, and flyers that looked like they escaped from a printer at 3 AM.

pair with: dance music
MALL

Mall-brand uniform

Graphic tees, cargo pockets, windbreakers, chokers, butterfly clips, slap bracelets, and the outfit logic of “meet by the fountain.”

pair with: Tech/Toys
MIN

Minimal cool

Slip dresses, simple tanks, center parts, monochrome, thin straps, and the cleaned-up magazine look that balanced all the neon noise.

pair with: movies + magazines
Y2K

Late-90s shine

Vinyl, metallics, translucent plastic, tiny sunglasses, cyber clubwear, and the belief that the future might have a dress code.

pair with: 1998 / 1999

Artifacts to collect

  • A mall photo-booth strip with one person blinking.
  • A magazine page folded to save an outfit.
  • A choker, a wallet chain, butterfly clips, or a bucket hat.
  • A shoe box covered in stickers and receipts.
