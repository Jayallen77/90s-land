Museum room briefing

In the 90s, technology became domestic folklore.

A beige tower in the den, a stack of free AOL CDs, a Tamagotchi beeping in class, a pager clipped to a belt, a Game Boy in the back seat — these were not just gadgets. They were rituals, status signals, chores, and tiny portals into the future.

What this zone is about

Tech got personal: family PCs, Windows 95, CD-ROM drives, Netscape, AOL, early online shopping, pagers, Game Boys, and chunky phones moved from specialist territory into ordinary life.

Toys became crazes: Pogs, Beanie Babies, Power Rangers, Tamagotchi, Pokémon cards, Polly Pocket, Super Soakers, Goosebumps books, and Happy Meal tie-ins turned playgrounds into trading floors.

The decade's design language started beige and button-heavy, then drifted toward translucent shells, neon accents, pocket devices, sticker-covered binders, and objects with personalities.
